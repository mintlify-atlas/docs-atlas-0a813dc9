Enters long when price closes above the upper band and short when below the lower band. Configurable standard deviation multiplier and lookback period.

<Note>
  This strategy is **disabled by default**. It is designed for high-volatility market conditions and should be tested on testnet and tuned to your target pairs before enabling in production.
</Note>

## Configuration

```json config.json
{
  "strategies": {
    "bollinger_breakout": {
      "enabled": false,
      "period": 20,
      "std_dev": 2.0
    }
  }
}
```

To enable the strategy, set `"enabled": true` in your `config.json`.

## Parameters

<ParamField path="enabled" type="boolean" default="false">
  Activates or deactivates the Bollinger Band Breakout strategy. Disabled by default — set to `true` to enable.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="period" type="integer" default="20">
  The lookback period for calculating the middle band (simple moving average) and the standard deviation. A value of 20 is the conventional default. Shorter periods make the bands more reactive to recent price; longer periods widen them and produce fewer signals.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="std_dev" type="number" default="2.0">
  The standard deviation multiplier used to set the upper and lower band distance from the middle band. The default of 2.0 means the bands encompass approximately 95% of recent price action. Increasing this value widens the bands and reduces the frequency of breakout signals; decreasing it narrows the bands and produces more frequent but lower-quality signals.
</ParamField>

## Signal logic

The strategy calculates three values on each candle:

- **Middle band** — the simple moving average over `period` candles
- **Upper band** — middle band + (`std_dev` × standard deviation)
- **Lower band** — middle band − (`std_dev` × standard deviation)

| Condition | Signal |
|---|---|
| Candle closes **above** the upper band | LONG |
| Candle closes **below** the lower band | SHORT |
| Candle closes inside the bands | NONE |

A close outside the band indicates that price has moved beyond its recent statistical range, suggesting a breakout with directional momentum.

<Note>
  Bollinger Band breakout signals work best in high-volatility environments where strong momentum follows a squeeze or consolidation period. In low-volatility or ranging markets, price can repeatedly touch and re-enter the bands without sustained follow-through, leading to false breakout signals. Monitor the `std_dev` setting — a higher multiplier such as 2.5 reduces noise in choppy conditions.
</Note>
