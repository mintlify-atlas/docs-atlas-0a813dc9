title: 'EMA Crossover Strategy'
description: 'Trend-following strategy that generates long and short signals based on fast and slow exponential moving average crossovers.'

---

Generates long signals when the fast EMA (default 9) crosses above the slow EMA (default 21), and short signals on the inverse. Best suited for trending markets on 15m–4h timeframes.

## Configuration

```json config.json
{
  "strategies": {
    "ema_crossover": {
      "enabled": true,
      "fast": 9,
      "slow": 21
    }
  }
}
```

## Parameters

<ParamField path="enabled" type="boolean" default="true">
  Activates or deactivates the EMA Crossover strategy. Set to `false` to disable without removing the configuration block.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="fast" type="integer" default="9">
  The lookback period for the fast exponential moving average. A smaller value makes the fast EMA more responsive to recent price changes.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="slow" type="integer" default="21">
  The lookback period for the slow exponential moving average. Must be greater than `fast`. A larger value produces a smoother, slower-reacting average.
</ParamField>

## Signal logic

The strategy computes two EMAs on the close price of each candle and compares their relative positions across consecutive bars.

| Condition | Signal |
|---|---|
| Fast EMA crosses **above** slow EMA | LONG |
| Fast EMA crosses **below** slow EMA | SHORT |
| No crossover detected | NONE |

A crossover is confirmed when the relationship between the two EMAs changes from one candle to the next — the fast EMA must be below the slow EMA on the previous bar and above it on the current bar (or vice versa for short signals).

## Recommended timeframes

The EMA Crossover strategy works best on intermediate timeframes where trends develop with enough structure to produce reliable crossovers:

- **15m** — faster signals, more frequent trades, higher noise
- **1h** — balanced between frequency and reliability
- **4h** — fewer signals, higher quality, lower noise

<Tip>
  This strategy performs best in trending markets with sustained directional momentum. In choppy or ranging conditions, the fast and slow EMAs will cross repeatedly without follow-through, generating false signals. Consider disabling or reducing position size during low-volatility consolidation phases.
</Tip>
